How to read this page
Ticker & Company
The stock code (e.g. MSFT) and the company behind it. The exchange shows where it trades — NASDAQ, NYSE, or ASX.
Signal — Long or Short
The model's prediction for tomorrow. ↑ Long = expects price to rise. ↓ Short = expects price to fall. Only signals at 60%+ confidence appear here.
Signal & Confidence
↑ Long indicates the model expects positive returns. Confidence is a Platt-calibrated probability — an ensemble mathematical representation of historical win rates.
Size (Half-Kelly)
The Kelly Criterion is a mathematical formula that calculates optimal bet size based on win probability. "Half-Kelly" divides this by 2 (an institutional standard) to aggressively minimize portfolio drawdowns while capturing upside edge.
Signal Streak
How many trading days in a row the model has made the same call. A 🔥 8-day streak is far more reliable than a brand new 1-day signal.
Trend (vs 10d Baseline)
Is the model getting more or less confident? ↑ means conviction is building versus the 10-day institutional baseline. ↓ means it's fading.
10-Day Average
The "Fortnightly Baseline." A 10-day window is the professional standard for filtering out daily noise and seeing the true structural conviction of the model.
